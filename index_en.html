The real country is not in news releases, but here.

What haven't we seen there?

According to VCIOM polls, 95% of Russians dream of going somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their vacation in their own country. Like, what haven’t we seen here at home? In fact, Russia is a whole universe with the gentle sea of the south, the dense forests of the Sayans, and the harsh ice of the Putorana Plateau. And all these beauties can be seen without millions in your account, a foreign passport, or hours of flights. Just like Vera Bashmakova — a brave young mother who took her three kids, packed them into her Lada, and drove 20,000 kilometers around her home country. We’ve selected and described some interesting places worthy of your vacation.

Curonian Spit

URL

park-kosa.ru

Here, among forests and sand dunes, you can see two water horizons — the calm Curonian Lagoon on one side and the rippling Baltic Sea on the other. A unique natural area on the edge of the Russian enclave.

And that’s not all of the Kaliningrad region. Nearby, there’s also the westernmost point of Russia — the Baltic Spit — and the German heritage of small seaside towns. The atmosphere of these places excludes hustle and bustle, immersing you in the calmness of nature and the smell of the steel-cold sea.

Kola Peninsula

URL

National Geographic

Almost the entire peninsula lies beyond the Arctic Circle. The Sámi tundra stretches south into taiga, and north into the Arctic Ocean, masquerading as the Barents Sea.

You might have seen Zvyagintsev’s films or heard about the Arctic festival in Teriberka. Maybe the word “Khibiny” didn’t get buried under your school geography memories. Maybe you haven’t heard about the deep borehole piercing the Earth’s crust, and apatite only brings apathy. But your dream of seeing the Northern Lights starts with a ticket to Murmansk.

Altai

URL

YouTube

Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia. Primarily because of the mountains: drive along the ridge, and you’ll see slopes covered with pines, mountain rivers, and lakes. Open your car window and meet the invisible miracle of these places — the mountain air.

The climate in Altai is temperate, so it’s best to go in summer. This way, you’ll see the full variety of local flora and fauna. Moose roam the forests, eagles soar above the ridges, and deer graze on the plains. The famous manuls also call Altai home.

Winter Baikal

URL

vk.com/baikalmile

Everyone knows Baikal as the largest lake in the world. Many also know it’s the largest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

True. But Baikal is also the perfect place for skijoring competitions. It’s a sport where a skier ties themselves to a motorcycle and the duo tries to reach maximum speed on the ice. In March 2019, the Baikal Mile festival set a world record — 197.011 km/h.

Karelia

URL

vodlozero.ru

Siberia doesn't end at the Urals, but in Karelia: the Siberian larch forming the taiga doesn't grow west of Lake Vodlozero. But here, it towers up to 30 meters — the forests of Karelia’s national parks have never seen an axe thanks to the impassable swamps. Some pines are more than 500 years old. Touch a living being that saw the sun before Ivan the Terrible. In the virgin forest, you won’t find a trail for a hundred kilometers. And on the rare paths, trees are clawed by bears — to mark their territory.
